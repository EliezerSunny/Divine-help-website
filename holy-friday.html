Good Friday, is the day Christians solemnly commemorate the crucifixion and death of Jesus Christ at Calvary. It is a day of reflection, repentance, and gratitude for the ultimate sacrifice made for humanity’s salvation.

This day falls within the Easter Triduum and is observed during the Holy Week, following Maundy Thursday and preceding Easter Sunday. It marks the fulfillment of prophecy and the depth of God’s love for mankind through the suffering of His Son.

On Good Friday, many believers participate in services that include the reading of the Passion of Christ, hymns, prayers, and moments of silence to honor the pain and sacrifice endured by Jesus.

Generally, this day is remembered as the moment Jesus was crucified, died, and was buried—leading to the glorious resurrection celebrated on Easter Sunday.
